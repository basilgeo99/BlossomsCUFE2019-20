About Blossoms

Blossoms,an Intra University Fest conducted by CHRIST(Deemed to be University) that begins as an Intra Departmental Cultural Fest, culminating into an Inter Deanery Competition. Blossoms’19 included four major events, Stage, Literary, Art & Theatre.

These four events were divided into 17 qualifying events namely: Indian Dance Classical Solo, Indian Dance Group (Non-Theme), Indian Electric Music Group, Western Electric Music Group, Western Acoustics Music Group, Pot Pourri, Quiz, Extempore, Debate, Dumb Charades, JAM, Street Theatre, Proscenium Theatre, Mime, Gift card making, Painting, Pot Art, Poster Painting & Cartooning.

General Guidelines

Department should ensure that all their students participate in minimum events.
The Students are allowed to participate in as many as events that they are interested to participate in depending on the schedule and timings of the events.
In this edition of Blossoms, the Faculty of Engineering and School of Architecture will be divided into 3 teams from the deaneries.
These are as follows :

  • 1. Science & Humanities
  • 2. Non-Circuit (Mechanical, Civil & Architecture)
  • 3. Circuit (CS/IT, EC, & EEE)

The events are divided into 4 categories for this edition of Blossoms

  • 1. Art
  • 2. Literary
  • 3. Stage
  • 4. Theatre

Department event coordinators should ensure that there should be equal participation in every event. Class teachers would play a key role in ensuring that all students of their class participate in at least one relevant event. Students absent for their assigned event must participate in any of the other following events to ensure 100% participation from the class.

The total points for BLOSSOMS at intra level is 400.

  • 1. Art & Literary events carry 5 points each -> 10 x 5 = 50 Points
  • 2. Stage Events carry 10 points each -> 5 x 10 = 50 Points
  • 3. Theater Events carry 20 points each - > 2 x 20 = 40 Points

260 points will be allotted by SWO for strategy, certification & participation

To ensure recognition, all students will be entitled for the participation certificates.

All Inter-deanery Finals are held on 5 February to 8 February, 2020 along with In Bloom 2020.

The decision of the Team/Deanery Event coordinator is final for the intra-deanery events.

Two of the best representations per event from each of the 9 deanery teams will be sent for the Finals.

As there are multiple participation opportunity this year, the deanery coordinators are requested to send the deanery contingents for each event according to the schedule and timing of the finale event.

The decision of SWO is final for the intra-department events.

Each Department Team will allotted Rs. 2500/- for all the expenditure till the finals of Blossoms.

Schedule of Strategy

  • 1. Publicity and Event organization (100 points)

    The Publicity is measured by visibility of paraphernalia as well as audience turn-out. Each department publicizes the event through whatever publicity material they wish to use, for example, banners, posters, flyers, street plays and other forms of campaigns during lunch hours. They need to ensure that they have a maximum strength audience for stage and theatre events. Class teachers must ensure that their entire class participates in the right event/s in consultation with the event coordinators. The format provided should ensure that event coordinators get all the required information from each class to collate all data. Attendance of each registered student must be accounted for to ensure full participation. The dean, HODs and teachers must ensure that there is 25% participation of students from their team/deanery in each of the four categories (Art, Lit, Stage & Theatre). This can be done by allotting a team of teachers to co-ordinate between departments and classes, to ensure equal distribution among events categories.

  • 2. Certification (10 points)

    Blossoms Certification Day for the Department must be ensured. The writing/signing of certificates and announcement of results on the deanery Certification day must be organized. Importance is also given to how the certification ceremony is made a special occasion for all students to receive the certificates from the respective event co-coordinators.

  • 3. Participation and Documentation (150 Points)

    Every department teams are requested to maintain records like participant per event, registration details, absentees list, result sheets, judges lists etc which will be checked by the SWO office at the Intra level evaluation time.100 Percent participation is expected from each department team.

JAM

  • > JAM master decides rounds.
  • > 8-9 participants are panelists who bang on the table if an error is committed, list out the error and speak if the JAM master agrees with the person.
  • > +5 for correct correction, -5 for wrong correction, +10 for every 5 seconds the participant speaks.
  • Additional points for getting the buzzer at the end of the minute. No participant is eliminated during the course of JAM.
  • Judged on clarity, content, presentation, relevance to the topic & language.
  • Participants lose points on late start, early start, speech defect, grammatical error.
  • > Time: 2:00 hr
  • > Top 2 participants will be selected for finals on 7 to 8 Feb, 2020
  • Dec 11, 2019
  • Block-2 Room-205
